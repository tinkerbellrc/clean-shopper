# Clean Shopper
## Demo Project Brief — Claude Code for Designers

---

## What It Is

Clean Shopper is an AI-powered agent that helps users discover home and pantry products that are clean, non-toxic, and environmentally friendly. Users can research products, compare options based on ingredient safety and sustainability, save their preferences, and build a shopping cart -- all through a conversational interface backed by Claude.

It is the course demo project for Claude Code for Designers. Students build it over four weeks using Claude Code, with the instructor demonstrating the full workflow live in each session.

---

## Target User

Someone who wants to make more informed purchasing decisions for their home and family -- replacing toxic cleaning products, finding personal care items without harmful chemicals, or stocking a pantry with cleaner food options -- but finds the research overwhelming and time-consuming.

Key frustrations the app addresses:

- Product labels are confusing and ingredient lists require expertise to interpret
- "Clean" and "natural" claims on packaging are often misleading or unverified
- Researching alternatives across multiple sources is fragmented and slow
- Preferences built up over time -- trusted brands, avoided ingredients -- have nowhere to live

---

## Feature Scope (V1)

**Product research**
Users describe what they're looking for and Clean Shopper researches options, evaluates ingredients against clean standards, and surfaces recommendations with reasoning. Claude searches the web in real time and draws on databases like EWG's Skin Deep for ingredient safety data.

**Preference management**
Users save their preferences: ingredients to avoid, trusted brands, certifications that matter to them (EWG Verified, USDA Organic, B Corp, etc.). Clean Shopper applies these to every subsequent recommendation automatically.

**Shopping cart**
Recommended products can be added to a cart that persists across sessions.

**Comparison**
Users can ask Clean Shopper to compare products side by side and get a clear recommendation based on their saved preferences.

Out of scope for V1: checkout or payment processing, direct retailer integrations, barcode scanning, user accounts or authentication, mobile app.
